About & Contact

A family story workspace built to feel more personal than genealogy software.

Ancestrio started as a practical way to understand real family connections, then grew into a project focused on browsing, preserving, and eventually printing those stories.

Why This Exists

Family history matters more when it becomes easier to revisit and share.

Family stories connect generations, but most tools make those stories feel like records to manage instead of relationships to understand. I built Ancestrio to make my own family history easier to browse, easier to preserve, and easier to turn into something tangible.

The demo tree uses real relationship patterns, and the product direction stays practical: one workspace for editing, one viewer that stays readable, and keepsakes that come from the same underlying data rather than a separate manual process.

What The Project Covers

Current focus

  • Interactive family tree viewer
  • Birthday calendar from stored dates
  • Journey globe from country data
  • Cloud-backed account workspace
  • Guest mode for local-only trials
  • Print keepsake ordering flow

Project Position

Open source and privacy-first

The code is public on GitHub, and the project does not exist to profile, sell, or trade family data.
